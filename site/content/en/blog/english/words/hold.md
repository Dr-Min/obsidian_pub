# hold: why can it mean grasp, keep, and contain?

Q. Why can `hold` mean grab something, keep an opinion, and contain people?

Many English words have several meanings.
If you look closely, those meanings often connect through one image.
`hold` is a very clear case.

## 1) Core Link at a Glance

- `hold` = grasp
- `hold` = keep
- `hold` = contain

Shared feeling: `keep something from slipping away`.

## 2) Step 1: Origin / Form

`hold` is an old basic word rather than a clean prefix-root word.
Its core image is to grasp, keep, or support something in place.
That is why you can `hold` an object, an idea, or a group of people in one space.

## 3) Step 2: Key Meanings

### ‚Ď† grasp

- This is the basic physical meaning.
- The shared image is keeping something in your hand.
- English: `Please hold my bag for a minute.`
- Meaning: Keep my bag in your hand for a moment.

### ‚Ď° keep

- You can `hold` a view, a record, or a position.
- The link is keeping something stable and not letting it go.
- English: `They still hold the same opinion.`
- Meaning: They still keep the same opinion.

### ‚ĎĘ contain

- A room, bottle, or box can `hold` something.
- The link is keeping things inside a limit.
- English: `This room can hold fifty people.`
- Meaning: This room has space for fifty people.

## 5) Wrap-up

The easiest image for `hold` is `keep it in place`.
If your hand keeps it, that is `hold`.
If your mind keeps it, that is also `hold`.
If a space keeps it inside, that is still `hold`.
