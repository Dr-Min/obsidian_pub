# cover: why can it mean protect, discuss, and pay for?

Q. Why can `cover` mean put something over an object, discuss a topic, and pay a cost?

Many English words have several meanings.
If you look closely, those meanings often connect through one image.
`cover` becomes easier when you keep the image of spreading over a whole area.

## 1) Core Link at a Glance

- `cover` = protect / put over
- `cover` = discuss
- `cover` = pay for

Shared feeling: `spread over a range and take it in`.

## 2) Step 1: Origin / Form

`cover` comes from a word meaning `put over` or `hide under a layer`.
That same image works for many modern senses.
You can cover a surface, cover a topic, or cover a cost.

## 3) Step 2: Key Meanings

### ‚Ď† protect / put over

- This is the physical meaning.
- The link is putting a layer over something.
- English: `Snow covered the road overnight.`
- Meaning: Snow spread over the road overnight.

### ‚Ď° discuss

- A chapter, class, or article can `cover` a topic.
- The link is including that topic within its range.
- English: `This chapter covers basic grammar.`
- Meaning: This chapter discusses basic grammar.

### ‚ĎĘ pay for

- A person or company can `cover` a cost.
- The link is taking responsibility over that expense.
- English: `My company covered the hotel bill.`
- Meaning: My company paid the hotel bill.

## 5) Wrap-up

The easiest image for `cover` is `spread over it`.
If a layer spreads over something, it means cover.
If an article spreads over a topic, it means cover.
If your money spreads over a cost, it also means cover.
