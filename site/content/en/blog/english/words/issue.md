# issue: why can it mean release, edition, and problem?

Q. Why can `issue` mean publish something, one magazine issue, and also a problem?

Many English words have several meanings.
If you look closely, those meanings often connect through one image.
`issue` gets easier when you think of something coming out into the open.

## 1) Core Link at a Glance

- `issue` = release
- `issue` = edition
- `issue` = problem

Shared feeling: `something comes out and becomes visible`.

## 2) Step 1: Origin / Form

`issue` = `ex` + `ire`

`ex` = out

`ire` = go

→ go out

That image helps with documents being issued, magazine issues coming out, and problems that come out into view.

## 3) Step 2: Key Meanings

### ① release

- Banks, governments, and companies can `issue` cards, passports, and statements.
- The link is sending something out.
- English: `The bank issued a new card.`
- Meaning: The bank released a new card.

### ② edition

- One published number of a magazine is an `issue`.
- The link is one thing that has come out.
- English: `I bought the latest issue of the magazine.`
- Meaning: I bought the newest published edition.

### ③ problem

- An `issue` can also be a matter that has come out and now needs attention.
- The link is something becoming visible in front of us.
- English: `Cost is still the biggest issue.`
- Meaning: Cost is still the biggest problem.

## 4) Step 3: Related Words / Patterns

- `issued` / [ˈɪʃuːd]
Meaning: released, officially given
Pattern: already sent out
Example: `The issued ticket was valid.`

- `reissue` / [ˌriːˈɪʃuː]
Meaning: issue again
Pattern: send out again
Example: `They reissued the book last year.`

- `issuer` / [ˈɪʃuːər]
Meaning: the person or institution that issues
Pattern: the source sending something out
Example: `Check the card issuer first.`

- `issuance` / [ˈɪʃuːəns]
Meaning: the act of issuing
Pattern: the process of sending out
Example: `Passport issuance takes time.`

- `exit` / [ˈeksɪt]
Meaning: way out, leave
Pattern: shares the `ex` image of going out
Example: `The exit is on your left.`

## 5) Wrap-up

The easiest image for `issue` is `come out`.
If a document comes out officially, it is issued.
If a magazine comes out, that is an issue.
If a hidden matter comes out into view, it becomes an issue.
