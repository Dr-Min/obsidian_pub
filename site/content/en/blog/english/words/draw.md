# draw: why can it mean pull, sketch, and attract?

Q. Why can `draw` mean pull a curtain, draw a picture, and draw attention?

Many English words have several meanings.
If you look closely, those meanings often connect through one image.
`draw` becomes much easier when you feel that image.

## 1) Core Link at a Glance

- `draw` = pull
- `draw` = sketch
- `draw` = attract / bring out

Shared feeling: `pull something forward`.

## 2) Step 1: Origin / Form

`draw` is another old basic word.
For learning, the most useful image is simple: `pull toward you`.
You pull a line across paper when you draw a picture, and you pull attention toward something when you draw interest.

## 3) Step 2: Key Meanings

### ‚Ď† pull

- This is the physical sense.
- The link is bringing something toward you.
- English: `She drew the curtain slowly.`
- Meaning: She pulled the curtain slowly.

### ‚Ď° sketch

- You `draw` when you make a picture with lines.
- The link is pulling lines across a surface.
- English: `My son drew a dragon on the wall.`
- Meaning: My son made a dragon picture on the wall.

### ‚ĎĘ attract / bring out

- A video can `draw` attention, and a question can `draw` an answer.
- The link is pulling something out or toward you.
- English: `The video drew a lot of attention online.`
- Meaning: The video attracted a lot of attention online.
