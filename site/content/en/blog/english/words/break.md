# break: why can it mean shatter, pause, and violate?

Q. Why can `break` mean shatter glass, take a break, and break the rules?

Many English words have several meanings.
If you look closely, those meanings often connect through one image.
`break` becomes much easier when you see one idea: stopping continuity.

## 1) Core Link at a Glance

- `break` = shatter
- `break` = pause
- `break` = violate

Shared feeling: `interrupt something that had been continuous`.

## 2) Step 1: Origin / Form

`break` is another old core word.
Its basic image is to split, interrupt, or destroy continuity.
That is why objects can break, work can break for a while, and rules can also be broken.

## 3) Step 2: Key Meanings

### ‚ëÝ shatter

- This is the physical meaning.
- The link is ending an intact whole.
- English: `Be careful not to break the glass.`
- Meaning: Be careful not to shatter the glass.

### ‚ë° pause

- A `break` is a short stop in work or study.
- The link is interrupting an ongoing flow.
- English: `Let's take a short break.`
- Meaning: Let's pause for a short time.

### ‚ë¢ violate

- You `break` a rule or promise by not keeping it.
- The link is interrupting the order that should continue.
- English: `Don't break the rules again.`
- Meaning: Do not violate the rules again.
