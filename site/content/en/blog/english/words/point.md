# point: why can it mean tip, key idea, and indicate?

Q. Why can `point` mean a sharp end, a main idea, and the action of pointing?

Many English words have several meanings.
If you look closely, those meanings often connect through one image.
`point` is easy to remember when you imagine marking one exact spot.

## 1) Core Link at a Glance

- `point` = tip / spot
- `point` = key idea
- `point` = indicate

Shared feeling: `mark one clear spot`.

## 2) Step 1: Origin / Form

`point` historically connects to ideas like piercing or pricking.
For learning, the simplest image is `a sharp exact point`.
That is why a tip is a point, the main idea is a point, and the act of indicating is to point.

## 3) Step 2: Key Meanings

### ① tip / spot

- This means a sharp end or an exact location.
- The link is a single exact place.
- English: `The pencil has a sharp point.`
- Meaning: The pencil has a sharp tip.

### ② key idea

- This means the main thing someone wants to say.
- The link is one exact idea being marked out.
- English: `That's a very important point.`
- Meaning: That is a very important key idea.

### ③ indicate

- This means direct attention toward something.
- The link is marking one place clearly.
- English: `She pointed at the map.`
- Meaning: She indicated a place on the map.

## 4) Step 3: Related Words / Patterns

- `pointer` / [ˈpɔɪntər]
Meaning: something that points
Pattern: the pointing tool or sign
Example: `Use the mouse pointer carefully.`

- `pinpoint` / [ˈpɪnˌpɔɪnt]
Meaning: identify exactly
Pattern: mark an even smaller point
Example: `We need to pinpoint the cause.`

- `pointed` / [ˈpɔɪntɪd]
Meaning: sharp, direct
Pattern: having a clear point
Example: `He asked a pointed question.`

- `viewpoint` / [ˈvjuːˌpɔɪnt]
Meaning: perspective
Pattern: the point from which you see
Example: `I understand your viewpoint.`

- `pointless` / [ˈpɔɪntləs]
Meaning: meaningless
Pattern: having no clear point
Example: `Arguing now is pointless.`

## 5) Wrap-up

The easiest image for `point` is `one exact spot`.
A sharp end is a point.
A key idea is a point.
The act of directing attention to one spot is also point.
