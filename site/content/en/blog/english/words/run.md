# run: why can it mean move, operate, and manage?

Q. Why can `run` describe a person, a machine, and a business?

Many English words have several meanings.
If you look closely, those meanings often connect through one image.
`run` is one of the clearest examples.

## 1) Core Link at a Glance

- `run` = move quickly
- `run` = operate
- `run` = manage

Shared feeling: `keep something moving without stopping`.

## 2) Step 1: Origin / Form

`run` is not a neat prefix-root-suffix word.
It is an old basic English word with a core image of movement and flow.
That is why people can `run`, machines can `run`, and stores can `run`.

## 3) Step 2: Key Meanings

### ① move quickly

- This is the basic physical meaning.
- It connects to the shared image because the body keeps moving forward.
- English: `She runs in the park every morning.`
- Meaning: She moves quickly on foot in the park every morning.

### ② operate

- Machines, programs, and engines can also `run`.
- The link is continuous internal movement.
- English: `The washing machine is still running.`
- Meaning: The machine is still operating.

### ③ manage

- A person can `run` a store, team, or project.
- The link is keeping a system in motion.
- English: `She runs a small bakery with her sister.`
- Meaning: She manages a small bakery with her sister.

## 4) Step 3: Related Words / Patterns

- `runner` / [ˈrʌnər]
Meaning: a person who runs
Pattern: `run + er`
Example: `The runner looked relaxed at the start line.`

- `running` / [ˈrʌnɪŋ]
Meaning: the act of running, or operating
Pattern: ongoing movement
Example: `We have running water again.`

- `rerun` / [ˈriːrʌn]
Meaning: run again, replay
Pattern: do the run one more time
Example: `They reran the show at night.`

- `outrun` / [ˌaʊtˈrʌn]
Meaning: run faster than
Pattern: move beyond another runner
Example: `You can't outrun the storm.`

- `runway` / [ˈrʌnweɪ]
Meaning: a strip for planes, or a fashion walkway
Pattern: a way made for running
Example: `The plane waited on the runway.`

## 5) Wrap-up

The easiest way to remember `run` is `keep moving`.
If a person moves, it means `run`.
If a machine keeps going, it means `run`.
If a system keeps working because someone controls it, that is also `run`.
