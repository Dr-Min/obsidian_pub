The core of this prompt is not simply "female warrior fights monsters." What really drives it is:

- a mythic establishing image with the red moon and ruined temple

## Original prompt

```text
Scene 1 (0-4s)
A lone female warrior in dark ceremonial armor stands inside a ruined stone temple beneath a massive red moon. Ash drifts through the air, broken banners flutter in the wind, and ancient stone pillars cast long shadows across the cracked ground. She slowly draws a glowing sword as the camera circles around her from a low angle. Mythic fantasy, cinematic lighting, dramatic shadows, epic atmosphere, ultra detailed.

Scene 2 (4-9s)
The warrior suddenly surges forward through the ruined temple at extraordinary speed, weaving between broken pillars and shadow-like enemies. As she moves, she exchanges rapid, precise strikes with them, brief flashes of steel and glowing sword trails cutting through the smoky air. Each clash is fast and fluid, with sparks, dust, and embers swirling around her feet as she defeats them without losing momentum. The camera races tightly alongside her in a dynamic side tracking shot, capturing the rhythm of short combat beats and relentless forward movement with cinematic motion blur. Epic fantasy action, fast choreography, powerful movement, intense momentum, high detail.

Scene 3 (9-13s)
Without slowing down, she continues upward through the collapsing temple, sprinting across shattered stone platforms while more shadowy enemies emerge in her path. She deflects and counters them in quick, elegant exchanges, never stopping, her glowing blade carving bright arcs through the dark ruins. Fragments of rock fall into the fiery abyss below, her cape and hair whip violently in the wind, and the enormous red moon fills the sky behind her. The camera sweeps upward with her ascent, combining a fast tracking motion with a dramatic rotating movement around her, emphasizing speed, vertical scale, and flowing combat. Grand mythic scale, cinematic fantasy, dynamic action, ultra detailed environment.

Scene 4 (13-15s)
At the highest edge of the shattered temple, the warrior launches into the air and raises her glowing sword toward a colossal horned beast emerging through smoke, moonlight, and drifting ash. The camera pulls back into a massive wide shot as both figures rush toward a final clash, ending in a brief, breathtaking moment of tension just before impact. Mythic climax, powerful silhouettes, cinematic fantasy, dramatic lighting, unforgettable ending.
```

## What the prompt is really doing

This is not just a chain of combat events. It is building a controlled escalation ladder:

1. establish mythic tone and visual authority
2. convert stillness into relentless horizontal momentum
3. turn that momentum upward while increasing danger and scale
4. stop on a high-tension pre-impact image instead of showing the hit

The real engine is the combination of `mythic environment`, `continuous movement`, `camera-driven speed`, and `a withheld final impact`.

## Why this structure works

### 1. The first scene locks the tone before the action begins

The red moon, drifting ash, ruined pillars, and glowing sword do most of the work before the warrior even moves. That gives the sequence a mythic register immediately instead of asking the action to build the world from scratch.

### 2. The combat is written as movement, not stoppage

The warrior does not stop to duel each enemy. She keeps advancing while striking. That matters because the sequence feels like a surge through space rather than a collection of disconnected fights.

### 3. The third scene upgrades both height and danger

The prompt does not repeat scene two on a different set. It adds collapsing platforms, a fiery abyss, stronger wind, more enemies, and a rotating ascent. That is what makes the short runtime feel larger than its actual length.

### 4. The ending holds the payoff instead of spending it

The final scene cuts just before impact. That gives the last frame more tension than a fully shown strike would, because the viewer is left at the exact moment of collision.

## Scene-by-scene breakdown

### Scene 1: establish mythic presence

- `dark ceremonial armor` sets silhouette and tone
- `ruined stone temple` gives the warrior a stage with history and scale
- `massive red moon` creates the dominant visual anchor
- `camera circles around her from a low angle` frames her as dangerous before movement begins

### Scene 2: turn the warrior into forward momentum

- `surges forward through the ruined temple` triggers the speed shift
- `weaving between broken pillars and shadow-like enemies` keeps the path visually busy
- `rapid, precise strikes` makes the fighting elegant rather than heavy
- `dynamic side tracking shot` turns the scene into a chase-like combat run

### Scene 3: convert speed into ascent

- `continues upward through the collapsing temple` adds vertical progression
- `shattered stone platforms` make each step feel unstable
- `glowing blade carving bright arcs` keeps the sword readable against the dark environment
- `fast tracking motion` plus `dramatic rotating movement` enlarges the scene scale

### Scene 4: frame the boss payoff

- `highest edge of the shattered temple` creates a final threshold
- `launches into the air` opens the climax with a clean silhouette
- `colossal horned beast` gives the sequence a final-scale opponent
- `massive wide shot` makes the last image feel mythic instead of merely action-heavy

## What to preserve when reusing this for Seedance 2.0

This is an inference from the prompt design rather than a benchmark claim.

- Open with a single dominant fantasy image before the first attack.
- Write combat as uninterrupted travel through space, not as stop-start duels.
- Escalate vertically in the middle section so the sequence expands in scale.
- End on a pre-impact wide shot when you want tension to linger after the clip ends.
