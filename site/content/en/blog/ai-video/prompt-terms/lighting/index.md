# Lighting

This page has been rebuilt with **real photographic references**.  
With lighting terms, the main question is not just what the light is called. It is **where the light is coming from**, **how soft or hard it feels**, and **how it separates the subject from the background**.

## soft light

Soft light creates gentle transitions between highlight and shadow. It usually feels smoother, kinder, and more flattering on skin and textured surfaces.

![soft light reference](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Comparison_of_softbox_to_direct_flash.jpg?width=960)

- Prompt fragment: `soft light, gentle shadow falloff, flattering skin texture`
- Real reference: [Comparison of softbox to direct flash.jpg](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Comparison_of_softbox_to_direct_flash.jpg)

## hard light

Hard light produces sharper shadow edges and stronger contrast. It is useful when you want drama, texture, or a more severe lighting shape.

![hard light reference](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Comparison_of_softbox_to_direct_flash.jpg?width=960)

- Prompt fragment: `hard light, crisp shadow edge, high-contrast texture`
- Real reference: [Comparison of softbox to direct flash.jpg](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Comparison_of_softbox_to_direct_flash.jpg)

## rim light

Rim light catches the edge of the subject and adds a line of glow around the silhouette. It is especially useful for separating hair and shoulders from the background.

![rim light reference](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Backlight_(50709183452).jpg?width=1100)

- Prompt fragment: `rim light catching the hairline, subject edge glow`
- Real reference: [Backlight (50709183452).jpg](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Backlight_(50709183452).jpg)

## backlight

Backlight comes from behind the subject. It can create silhouette, glow, separation, and a strong sense of atmosphere.

![backlight reference](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Backlight-wedding.jpg?width=1100)

- Prompt fragment: `strong backlight, semi-silhouette portrait, luminous edge separation`
- Real reference: [Backlight-wedding.jpg](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Backlight-wedding.jpg)

## key light

The key light is the main source shaping the face or subject. It defines the dominant side of the lighting pattern.

![key light reference](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Photographic_studio_setup.jpg?width=900)

- Prompt fragment: `key light from camera right, studio portrait setup`
- Real reference: [Photographic studio setup.jpg](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Photographic_studio_setup.jpg)

## fill light

Fill light does not replace the key. It reduces shadow density so important information is not lost in darkness.

![fill light reference](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Photographic_studio_setup.jpg?width=900)

- Prompt fragment: `subtle fill light, controlled shadow recovery, balanced contrast`
- Real reference: [Photographic studio setup.jpg](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Photographic_studio_setup.jpg)

## practical lighting

Practical lighting uses visible light sources that exist inside the shot, such as lamps, bulbs, screens, or candles.

![practical lighting reference](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Floor_lamp_near_a_wall_(Unsplash).jpg?width=1000)

- Prompt fragment: `practical lighting from a floor lamp, cozy interior glow`
- Real reference: [Floor lamp near a wall (Unsplash)](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Floor_lamp_near_a_wall_(Unsplash).jpg)

## neon glow

Neon glow adds colored light spill and reflected artificial atmosphere. It is one of the fastest ways to build an urban night look.

![neon glow reference](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Red_and_blue_open_neon_(Unsplash).jpg?width=1200)

- Prompt fragment: `red and blue neon glow, reflective surfaces, cyberpunk night mood`
- Real reference: [Red and blue open neon (Unsplash)](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Red_and_blue_open_neon_(Unsplash).jpg)

## golden hour

Golden hour is the warm low-angle sunlight near sunset. It tends to flatter skin and gives scenes a more romantic or cinematic softness.

![golden hour reference](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Golden_Hour_(Unsplash).jpg?width=1200)

- Prompt fragment: `golden hour sunlight, warm rim highlights, soft sunset atmosphere`
- Real reference: [Golden Hour (Unsplash)](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Golden_Hour_(Unsplash).jpg)

## blue hour

Blue hour is the cool ambient light just after sunset. It works especially well for city skylines, waterfronts, and reflective night scenes.

![blue hour reference](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Blue_Hour_(196206709).jpeg?width=1200)

- Prompt fragment: `blue hour skyline, cool ambient light, calm evening tone`
- Real reference: [Blue Hour (196206709).jpeg](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Blue_Hour_(196206709).jpeg)

## volumetric light

Volumetric light makes beams visible in the air. It becomes stronger when haze, dust, or moisture gives the light something to travel through.

![volumetric light reference](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Sunrays_through_the_trees_(Unsplash).jpg?width=1200)

- Prompt fragment: `volumetric light beams, visible sun rays through haze`
- Real reference: [Sunrays through the trees (Unsplash)](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Sunrays_through_the_trees_(Unsplash).jpg)

## Summary

Lighting terms become much stronger when combined instead of used one by one.

- flattering commercial portrait: `soft light + key light + subtle fill`
- silhouette or separation: `backlight + rim light`
- urban synthetic night mood: `neon glow + hard light`
- natural time-of-day mood: `golden hour` or `blue hour`

In AI video prompting, lighting is often the fastest lever for shifting both mood and color at the same time.
